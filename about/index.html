About

I am a fourth year undergradute at Indian Institute of Technology-Kanpur. I am majoring in Electrical Engineering, and minoring in Computer Science.

I developed a lot of interest in Computer Vision in my second year, when I happened to play around with OpenCV for the first time, while working on a landmark-based indoor localisation problem. Since then I have worked on numerous other problems including Optical Flow, Range Flow (the 3D extension of optical flow), and Visual Odometry. I then started exploring Machine Learning to solve problems in Computer Vision, explored it via course projects and Kaggle competitions. I spent the summer of 2015 at the Computer Science Department of Cornell University, where I worked under Prof. Ashutosh Saxena on the Brain4Cars project, and closely collaborated with Ashesh Jain on the same. I still continue to work on the project here at IIT-Kanpur.

I was the coordinator of Electronics Club for the year 2014-15, and led a team of 15 club secretaries to conduct lectures, workshops, competitions. I floated and mentored numerous projects in the field of Embedded Systems, Human-Computer Interaction, FPGAs, and Computer Vision.

I recently started blogging on this site, and I have also written a post for LearnOpenCV.com. I enjoy reading, and here is my goodreads.
