About

I am presently in the the third of year of my undergraduate studies at Indian Institute of Technology-Kanpur. I am majoring in Electrical Engineering, and am also pursuing a minor in Computer Science.

I developed a lot of interest in Computer Vision in my second year, when I happened to play around with openCV for the first time, while working on a landmark-based indoor localisation problem. Since then I have worked on numerous other problems including Optical Flow, Range Flow(the 3D extension of optical flow), and Visual Odometry. I am particulary interested in working with RGB-D data obtained from structured light and time-of-flight sensors.

I am also the coordinator of Electronics Club at IIT-Kanpur, and am leading a team of 15 club secretaries to conduct lectures, workshops, competitions. I also float and mentor numerous projects in the field of Embedded Systems, Human-Computer Interaction, FPGAs, and Computer Vision.

In my free time, I design and build electronics, write down some small hacks, and read research papers, editorials, academic blogs and, of course, fiction.
